British Learning Disability Championships – Swansea - 22/23 August 2015

Five swimmers attended the British Learning Disability Championships held at the Welsh National Pool in Swansea on 22nd and 23rd August.

It was a very inspiring meet with Jessica-Jane Applegate and Jack Thomas in attendance. Some lucky swimmers got the chance opportunity to race against them. Jessica-Jane starred in the London 2012 Paralympics, winning gold in the S14 200m Freestyle setting a new Paralympic and British record and has just won 2 silver medals at the IPC World Championships in Glasgow. Jack Thomas picked up the S14 200m Freestyle at the British Championships in Glasgow and also went on to gain medals at both the Glasgow 2014 Commonwealth Games and IPC European Championships. 

It was a fantastic weekend for the Bluefins’ swimmers and it was clear to see that their summer training paid off - 17 races, 17 PBs and 5 speeding tickets with a total of 4.5 minutes being knocked off their PBs. Worth of a particular mention:

Jamie Joyce - has shown an improved stroke which has led to PBs in all events and knocking an amazing 10 seconds off his 25m Freestyle.

Joe Pavey - Smashed his 25m Freestyle, gaining 2 speeding tickets and PBs in all events.

Cameron Lloydd - had a flying start and finish on his 50m Freestyle gaining a speeding ticket.

Scott Howe - has worked hard during training and this has clearly paid off with PBs being obtained in every race.

Ciarán Kelly - did one tough race after another gaining PBs in every race and has improved his race technique knocking an incredible 58 seconds off his 800m Freestyle PB.

Ciarán finished the weekend off by being the anchor swimmer in the unattached relay team (made up from various teams) gaining Bronze in the 50m Medley and Silver in the 50m Freestyle which is an amazing achievement as these swimmers don’t train with each other.
