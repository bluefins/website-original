South East Regional LC Championships

Eight bluefins travelled to Crawley this weekend for the South East Regional long course championships.

James Scott opened day 1 for Bluefins with a terrific 200 Freestyle. Coached for the day by dad Tony, he followed all race tactics and had an outstanding swim with a great PB. He even managed a wave from the block when he heard his team mates cheering. He then went on to do another good swim in the 50 free. Not to be outdone by his son, Tony then went on and swam a great 50 fly with a superb PB.

Mark Straw had a great day with silver in the 200 free, missing the gold buy a second and an outstanding swim to win gold in the 800 free. Spencer Turner swam superbly to gold in the 200 free and silver in the 50 free. Neil Mitchell pulled out 2 PBs in the 50 free and 100 breast, he also won bronze for the 100 breast.

Not to be outdone, the ladies team were also in the medals. Catherine Shearn did a brilliant 100 fly to win gold. Michelle Matson, plagued with injuries still won a bronze for the 100 breast. Fleur kent won gold in the 50 free and 200 back.

Day 2 was opened by Neil Mitchell in the 400 IM winning silver and another PB. He then went on to get another best time and bronze for the 50 breast and silver In the 200 breast. Spencer Turner pulled out a PB to win silver in the 100 free, followed by silver in the 50 breast and 5th in the 50 back with a very hard finish! Mark Straw, still pleased with his excellent swims from day 1, swam another good race in the 100 free, just missing out on the medals. Then went on to win the 400 Freestyle in another great time.

Again the ladies were up for the challenge seeing Michelle Matson pulling out more medals with gold in the 200 breast and 50 Fly and silvers in the 50 back and 50 breast. Catherine Shearn won the 50 fly to get another gold and swam well for a bronze in the 400 Freestyle. Fleur Kent finished the weekend off with 4 more Gold’s in the 50 back, 50 breast, 100 free and 100 Back

Overall an excellent Regional championships for Bluefins with their best team results of 13 Gold's 6 Silvers and 4 Bronze medals.
