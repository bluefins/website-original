ASA South East Region Winter Championships 2015

Last weekend saw swimmers from across the South East Region compete in the annual winter championships short course competition. Qualification criteria is especially tough for this event as there is only one qualifying time for all age groups, so predominantly the swimmers were made up of older youth and senior swimmers.

The region is made up of six counties, ranging from Hampshire in the south, right up to Oxford and North Buckinghamshire.

The programme promised some excellent racing, and all the spectators in attendance were in for a real treat, seeing some of the top talent in the area battle it out for top honours. Two of those talented swimmers attending the competition were Basingstoke Bluefins’ Molly Goffin aged 15 and 16 year old Mitchell Coombes, both members of the clubs Performance Squad.

As the competition is placed still fairly early on in the season, swimmers are not only looking to swim fast, but just as importantly, to hone their skills and techniques to stand them in good stead for the competitions which lie ahead next year.

Molly competed in the 100 and 200 metres Breastroke, and also the 100 and 200 metres Backstroke. Technically, Molly was very strong, her underwater parts of the races are an area where she can excel against the other competitors, and she used this to great advantage. Her 200 metre Breastroke race particularly paid testament to this, as she gained up to a metre on some competitors just coming off the wall out of a turn!

Mitchell was also showing great form, competing in all three Breastroke events, the 50, 100 and 200 metre events. He showed superb pacing skills, particularly in the 200m event, where his last three 50 metre split times varied by less than 1.5 seconds, with the last split time being just faster than the previous two. One of Mitchell’s goals for the weekend was to put into practice the work done in training on his underwater pull-out phase after each turn. This he certainly did to great effect, and was no doubt a contributing factor in him being able to pace consistently, whilst some other competitors fell by the wayside as it were, shortening there underwater phases considerably as they tired throughout the race.

All in all these two Bluefins swimmers did the team proud, and both gained three lifetime personal best times along the way, which is no mean feat this early on in the competition calendar, so a big well done to both of them from all at the club!

Bluefins are next in action on 21/22 November competing at a Level 2 meet in Bristol and a Level 3 meet in Swindon. These are the last opportunities for swimmers to qualify for ASA Winter Nationals Short Course Meet.
